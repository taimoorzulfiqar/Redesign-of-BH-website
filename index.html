Subsidiary Companies

Berkshire Hathaway is a testament to the investment philosophy of its Chairman and CEO, Warren E. Buffett.

A holding company with a value-oriented approach, Berkshire Hathaway has grown over the decades from a textile company into a diverse portfolio of wholly-owned businesses and significant minority investments.

  • Berkshire seeks out companies with durable competitive advantages, strong management, and attractive valuations.
  • Berkshire does not emphasize short-term earnings, allowing our businesses to reinvest profits and build lasting value for shareholders.
  • Our decentralized approach grants each subsidiary the freedom to operate independently, guided by a core set of principles.

Our primary businesses operate in insurance, railroads, and energy, providing essential services across the United States and beyond. Berkshire Hathaway owns and operates a multitude of diverse companies, encompassing sectors such as manufacturing, retail, media, services, and more.

Long-term Investment Philosophy

Berkshire Hathaway focuses on acquiring quality businesses to hold for extended periods, emphasizing sustainable growth and long-term value creation for shareholders.

Financial Strength and Stability

With substantial cash reserves and a strong track record, Berkshire Hathaway provides financial backing and stability to its subsidiaries, allowing them to weather economic downturns and pursue strategic investments.

Decentralized Management

Berkshire Hathaway trusts its subsidiary leaders. This decentralized approach fosters operational autonomy, entrepreneurial spirit, and long-term decision-making within individual companies.

Diversification and Investment Opportunities

Berkshire Hathaway's diverse holdings offer shareholders exposure to various industries and reduce overall portfolio risk. Additionally, the company's significant cash position allows it to capitalize on attractive investment opportunities as they arise
